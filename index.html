Why are we doing this study?
You are invited to participate in a research study entitled “Assessing the Environmental Impact of the Canadian Medical Residency Interview Tour”. This study is being done by Dr. Simon Donner, a researcher from the Department of Geography, University of British Columbia (UBC), and Kevin Liang a fourth-year medical student from UBC. You are invited because of your participation in the first iteration of the 2019-2020 medical residency interview tour (commonly known as CaRMS©).
The purpose of this research study is to quantify CO2 emissions generated from air travel related to the interview tour. We believe this national survey of medical students will give us a better understanding of the environmental impact of this process.

Who can participate in this study?
The study population comprises of graduating students from the 17 Canadian medical school who are taking part in the first iteration of the 2019-2020 residency interview tour. There is no exclusion criterion.

What happens if you say “Yes, I want to be in the study”?
If you decide to participate in this study, you will be linked to an online survey lasting approximately 10 minutes that can be completed at a single timepoint. At the start of the survey, you will see a field to input the city where you started your residency interview tour. You can enter subsequent cities you travelled to. Please indicate whether you travelled to these cities by airplane using the drop-down menu. After completion, please click on the “Calculate My Emissions” button. The webpage will then guide you to a section that shows
(1) The number of cities you visited during the process
(2) The total distance travelled (reported in kms)
(3) The total CO2 emitted (reported in tonnes of CO2e)
Our calculations assume that each trip is economy class. To calculate distance between individual departure city and destination city, we use the greater circle distance, which is the shortest distance between two points on a sphere. To calculate emissions, we use a conversion factor derived from the UK Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS) (Table 1).

After seeing these results, you will have the option of submitting your data through a survey platform hosted by Google Form, embedded on the website. You can enter the name of your medical school and the specialty(ies) that you applied to. Only when you hit the “submit” button will the following information be collected:

If you wish to withdraw from the study at any point while completing the survey, simply close your web browser, and your data will not be collected.

Will being in this research study help you in any way?
You may not directly benefit from this research; however, data collected from the study will allow us to understand the environmental impact of the residency tour.

What are the possible risks or inconveniences of participating?
We believe there are minimal risks associated with this research study; however, a risk of breach of confidentiality always exists and we have taken the steps to minimize this risk as outlined in the section below entitled “How will your personal information be protected?”.
You are free to decline to answer any questions you do not wish to answer or stop participating at any time by closing your browser. If you close your browser before getting to the end of the survey or you do not click the ‘submit’ button at the end of the survey your information collected up to that point will not be collected nor used.

How will your personal information be protected?
To the best of our ability your answers in this study will remain confidential. Only your medical school, the cities you travelled to during the residency tour, and the speciality(ies) you applied to will be collecUted. This may be a limit to your confidentiality, as we believe that it is possible for participants to be identified non-directly. However, only the research investigators (Dr. Simon Donner and Kevin Liang) will have access to this information.

Data Management, Storage, and Destruction
The data collected on you for this research project will be stored in an encrypted server hosted by Google Form, a third-party survey platform. The Google Form is attached only to the researchers’ UBC emails. These servers are located outside of Canada (United States and Europe). If you choose to participate in the survey, you understand that your responses to the survey questions may be stored and accessed in the USA. As a result, it is subject to U.S. laws, in particular, the US Patriot Act which allows authorities access to the records of internet service providers. The security and privacy policy for the web survey company can be found at the following link: https://policies.google.com/privacy.
The data stored through Google Form server is both password-protected and encrypted. To further protect the data, we will remove any direct personal information provided (e.g. Name, Date of Birth). Google Form employs multiple layers of security to make sure that data remains private and secure. This data is only accessible by team members Kevin Liang and Dr. Simon Donner and will only be accessed in Canada. The data will be stored for 5 years from the date of study completion (June 30th, 2020) and will be completely deleted from the drive subsequently by Kevin Liang. All Google Form accounts are protected by password-access, and Google employees do not have access the responses. All responses will be anonymous, Google Form does not have the option to collect your IP addresses.

Future Use of Data
Your responses will be used to estimate the total amount of carbon dioxide emitted by medical students participating in this interview process. Your data will not be used for further analysis at the conclusion of the study.

Sharing of Study Results
The results of this study will be reported in a research piece and may be published in journal articles and reports. All report findings will be anonymous and cannot be linked back to you. A summary of the study results will be provided to you upon request. Please see above for contact information.

Will You be Paid for Taking Part?
You will not be provided with any payments or coverage of costs for participating in this study.
